Leonato 欢迎 Don Pedro 和他的随从 Benedick、Claudio，以及他同父异母的弟弟 Don John 来到自己家中。Beatrice 和 Benedick 一见面就斗嘴争吵，而 Claudio 则表达了自己对 Hero 的爱意。

Act 4: Wedding, Public Shaming, and Hidden Truth

第四幕：婚礼、公开羞辱与真相潜伏

Scene 1

The Wedding Disaster 婚礼灾难现场

Claudio publicly accuses Hero of unfaithfulness at the altar. Hero collapses, but Friar Francis comes to her rescue by proposing a plan. A deceptive plan is made to conceal Hero and give out that she has died of grief. Beatrice demands that Benedick prove his loyalty and they fall in love.

在婚礼上，Claudio当众指责Hero对他不忠。Hero当场晕倒，但Friar Francis挺身而出，提出了一个应对计划。于是众人制定了一场“善意的骗局”：先将Hero藏起来，并对外宣称她因悲伤过度而死。与此同时，Beatrice要求Benedick证明他对自己的忠诚，而两人也终于彼此确认了爱意。

Scene 2

Dogberry的守夜人终于揭露了Borachio的供词，也曝光了Don John的恶行。

Comic teaser: 🕵️‍♂️🫢 The clowns solve the case.

在这部戏最温柔动人的时刻之一，虽然他们依旧保持着那种机智逗趣的说话风格，但Beatrice 和 Benedick 终于承认了彼此的爱意。

以为 Hero 已经死去的 Claudio 公开表达了自己的哀悼与悔恨，并为 Hero 写下了一篇墓志铭。

Hero 还活着的真相终于被揭晓，一切真相也随之恢复清白，而全剧最终在欢庆与婚礼中落下帷幕。

Comic teaser: 🎉💍🎭 Everybody gets closure and dancing.

Characters 角色

Major character cards for revision.

Beatrice

Role: Hero's Cousion

Beatrice is brilliant, funny, independent, and emotionally courageous. She speaks and behaves with more than usual freedom, and her witty intelligence presents a challenge to all the male characters. Her name, like that of Benedick, means 'the blessed one', and she too has sworn never to marry.

Beatrice聪明出众、风趣幽默、独立自主，而且在感情上也十分勇敢。她说话和行事都比一般人更自由大胆，而她机敏犀利的智慧也对所有男性角色构成了挑战。她的名字和Benedick一样，都有“受祝福的人”之意，而她也同样发誓终身不嫁。

witty 机智的 sharp-tongued 言语犀利的 playful banter 有趣的 intelligent 聪慧的 independent 独立的 morally brave 勇敢

Benedick

Role: Don Pedro's follower

Benedick adopts a pose of woman-hater, and swears he will never marry. As a comic bachelor who begins by mocking love, then grows into someone loyal, sincere, and refashioned.

Benedick 摆出一副厌恶女人的姿态，并发誓自己绝不结婚。作为一个喜剧式的单身汉，他起初嘲笑爱情，后来却逐渐成长为一个忠诚、真诚，并在爱情中完成自我改变的人。

playful 搞笑的 proud 骄傲的 loyal 忠诚的 sarcastic 讽刺的 Fear of being made a "cuckold" 害怕被戴绿帽子 refashioned 人设有转变的

Hero

Role:The host daughter

Hero is described by Claudio as 'a modest young lady', and dismissed by Benedick as 'too low for a high praise, and too little for a great praise'. She is gentle and quiet, but central to the play’s exploration of innocence, female reputation, and public shame.

Hero被Claudio 形容为 “一位娴静端庄的年轻小姐”，而Benedick则评价她 “还不值得用太高的赞美来称颂，也太不起眼，不配受盛大的夸奖”。她温柔、安静，却处于整部戏对纯真、女性名誉与公开羞辱这些主题探讨的核心位置。

gentle 温柔的 obedient 顺从的 wronged 被误会了 passive 被动的 chaste and virtuous 贞洁且有美德

Claudio

Role: A young noble man

He has been honoured for his conduct in the recent fighting, but is still very immature and naive in his judgement. He is romantic but impulsive. His flaws reveal how honour, masculinity, and public judgment can become destructive.

他因在最近的战斗中表现出色而受到赞誉，但在判断事情时仍然十分不成熟，也很天真。他富有浪漫情怀，却又冲动鲁莽。他的缺点揭示了：当荣誉、男性气概和公众评判被推向极端时，它们也会变成具有破坏性的力量。

romantic 浪漫的 impressionable 令人影响深刻的 naive and quick to judge 天真且嘴快 flawed 有缺陷的

Don Pedro

Role: The Prince of Aragon

Don Pedro is powerful and charming. He has won a victory over his illegitimate brother Don John, and is visiting Messina on his way home. He helps create the comedy, but also joins the false judgment against Hero.

Don Pedro权势显赫、风度迷人。他刚刚战胜了自己同父异母的弟弟Don John，并在返乡途中到访Messina。他推动了剧中的喜剧情节发展，却也参与了对Hero的错误指责。

charismatic 迷人的 powerful 强壮的 matchmaker 大媒人 Seeks order and harmony 寻求秩序与和谐

Don John

Role: villain

Don John is the illegitimate brother of Don Pedro. He describes himself as "a plain-dealing villian". Driven by resentment and alienation, Don John uses deception to ruin happiness and social harmony.

Don John是Don Pedro同父异母的弟弟。他把自己形容为 “一个直来直去的恶棍”。在怨恨与疏离感的驱使下，Don John利用欺骗去破坏他人的幸福与社会的和谐。

malicious and deceitful 恶意且狡诈 dishonest 不诚信的 creates conflict 制造冲突 trouble-maker 捣蛋鬼

Leonato

Role:The Governor of Messina, Hero's father

He is flattered by the attentions of Don Pedro, and quick to believe whatever the prince tells them. He is a paternal authority figure whose responses reflect the pressures of honour, reputation, and social expectation.

他因Don Pedro对他的礼遇而感到受宠若惊，也很快就相信亲王告诉他的一切。他是一位父权式的权威人物，而他的反应也体现出荣誉、名声与社会期待所带来的压力。

respected 受人尊敬的 paternal 爹味儿老登

Dogberry

Role: Master Constable of Messina

Dogberry is absurd, confident, and linguistically chaotic. He has a great opinion of himself and his office but has an imperfect of the English language. He accidentally becomes one of the most important truth-finders in the play.

Dogberry荒唐可笑、自信满满，而且说话常常颠三倒四、混乱不清。他对自己和自己的职位评价极高，却并不能很好地掌握英语。可偏偏就是这样一个人，阴差阳错地成了全剧中最重要的真相揭露者之一。

ridiculous 离谱的 comic 喜剧人 malaprop-prone 很容易闹词语笑话的 useful chaos 制造有用的混乱

The World of the Play 剧中的世界

Exam Themes:可能考的主题
1. The World of the play 剧中的世界 -> Messina

Thesis Statement: In Much Ado About Nothing, the world of the play is set in a small town named Messina, which is full of outside and inside conflicts. The battlefield drives the outside conflict of Messina, while the internal conflicts of Messina are more complex, driven by a merry war, a gender war, and a social class war.

Essay Plan:

Outside of Messina -> battlefield -> full of conflicts dominated by males
Inside of Messina ->
Merry War:
1) Beatrice & Benedick -> oral argument with witty language
2) Claudio & Hero -> conflict around betrayal/ trust
Gender War:
1) patriarchal society -> women are deemed naturally unchaste 女性被认为是天生就不贞洁 -> men must protect their honour by any means necessary
2) Men in this play have an innate fear of cuckoldry. 男性都害怕被戴绿帽子
Class war: Prince (Don Pedro) -> Host (Leonato) -> Signor (Benedick) -> Count (Claudio) ->Hero/Beatrice (fair lady) -> Servant (Dogberry/Borachio/Conrade) & Maid (Ursula & Margret)
Truth is controlled by the nobility - Don Pedro has the absolute rightness? -> the servant’s words - why Leonato does not believe in Dogberry’s word? 1) Dogberry’s malapropism causes misunderstanding 2) Dogberry’s class is inferior to others -> ironically, he speaks the truth - Hero is being fashioned by others.
Nobility 贵族-> the 角色名字 Don Pedro rules the society and has power -> Leonato believe what Don Pedro says about Hero, even if it is not true.
Servant 仆人 -> Servants are considered inferior -> Leonator does not care what Dogberry says.

Love and Marriage 爱情与婚姻

2. Love and Marriage

TS: 观众更倾向于celebrate B&B‘s love rather than C&H’‘s love - 用fashion&deception来串
Love and marriage are compared to a war
Love and Marriage between Hero & Claudio
Claudio describes how he first "look' d upon her [Hero] with a soldier's eye" (1.1.275), but love has allowed him to desire a wife, especially now that the fighting is over. 克劳迪奥描述了他最初是如何 “用士兵的眼光看着她（希罗）”（1.1.275），但爱情让他渴望拥有一位妻子，尤其是现在战斗已经结束。
Been through misunderstanding, miscommunication caused by Don John’s deception -> conflict & public shaming
The audience tends not to celebrate C&H because it is full of conflict!!!!
Comedy convention -> The relationship between Hero and Claudio follows the comedy convention, which starts with a mistaken identity (Act 2 S1 Don Pedro pretends to be Claudio to woo Hero) -> Misunderstanding (Hero’s chastity was fashioned by Don John) [Why? - Don John is jealous of Claudio’s honour] -> Conflict (Hero’s and Leonato’s public honour being harmed) -> Resolution/Reconciliation (Hero’s fake death figuratively symbolises the rebirth, the honour outweighs the truth) -> Happy Ending
Appearance & Reality -> fashion’s impact on marriage and love, Hero’s identity is always being fashioned
Love and Marriage between Beatrice & Benedick
The root of Beatrice and Benedick's 'war of wits' is their great love for each other, which they deny, due to their public disdain for love and marriage. “斗智斗勇 ”的根源在他们公开蔑视爱情和婚姻，他们否认彼此的爱
Been through a trickery led by Don Pedro, they refashion to someone accepted love
Audiences tend to celebrate B&B -> they are playful, true love, align with comedy traditions.

三段：
BP1 写Claudio & Hero 的爱情和婚姻- Hero 是怎么被fashioned by others，Hero的贞洁是怎么影响Claudio的，经历了什么冲突误解，结局怎么样
BP2 写Benedick & Beatrice 的爱情和婚姻 - 怎么self-fashion，因为什么经历了refashion，对比refashion前后对于恋爱和婚姻观的区别
BP3 写为什么观众更喜欢BB而不是CH，BB更搞笑，台词dialogue更witty，没有那么多冲突，是true love。CH很多冲突，没有什么角色成长-观众会尤其对Claudio失望，因为他一直都很容易相信别人而不是相信Hero

Appearance vs Reality 外表与真实

3. Appearance VS Reality 外表与真实
用self-fashion, Refashion, Fashion by others 串

1) 角色是怎么用Self-fashion 塑造自己人物的外表“mask”
Benedick -> confirmed bachelor who dislikes love and marriage because his definition of honour is constructed by Messina’s values (Battlefield honour + not being cuckolded) -> maintains his honour
Beatrice -> an independent lady who does not believe in love -> independence -> Sceptic to the constructed patriarchal value of Messina, also because of her insecurity of being loved -> All men are deceivers
Don John -> appearance = reality -> plain-dealing villain -> straighforwardness
2) 角色人物形象的变化refashion
Benedick -> from a mean man to a gentleman who fights for his own love
Beatrice -> who hides her insecurity to show her insecurity to Benedick and believes in love
B&B’s refashion is due to trickery -> well-intended deception can lead to a good outcome -> let the character’s self-discover their own reality
Claudio -> who is a noble young man with honour to a hypocrite who public shames women and show no regrets.
Don Pedro -> a noble prince who is the matchmaker to a man who spreads rumors
3) 被造谣的角色 Fashioned by others
Hero -> young, sweet lady with good reputation -> fashioned by Don John as “disloyal”, fashioned by Claudio as “rotten orange” -> fashioned by Leonato as “dead”
In a patriarchal society like Messina, women's appearance is always fashioned by men of a higher class, like Don Pedro. However, reality cannot be fashioned; it relies on truth. Deception will be defeated.

Honour 荣誉

4. Honour 荣誉

3 types of males’ honour:
Social status 社会地位, whether won on the battlefield
Honour to protect one’s reputation -> Claudio and Don Pedro publicly shame Hero for her questionable loyalty, to protect their honour and reputation
Honour to protect the weak and vulnerable (i.e., being a gentleman)-> Benedick challenges Claudio to a duel to defend Hero's honour, out of love and loyalty for Beatrice, at the cost of his friendships with Don Pedro and Claudio

Female’s honour:
In this play, women were expected to be chaste and virtuous -> being a maid
Women‘s reputation = the whole family’s reputation
Women‘s reputation is totally controlled by men with power -> fashion

Trickery & Deception 恶作剧与欺骗

5. Trickery & Deception 恶作剧与欺骗

TS: Well-intended deception will lead to a healthier relationship, an audience-preferred outcome & evil-intended deception will lead to misunderstanding and conflict that the audience tends not to celebrate.
Examples：
Act 2 Scene 1 - Don Pedro pretends to be Claudio to woo Hero;
Act 2 Scene 1- Don John tells Claudio that Don Pedro woos for himself;
Act 2 Scene 1 - Beatrice pretends she doesn't know Benedick is behind the mask at the ball and insults him;
Act 2 Scene 1 - Don Pedro creates a plan to bring Beatrice and Benedick together;
Act 3 Scene 2 - Borachio and Don John make Claudio and Don Pedro believe Hero is unchaste;
Act 4&5- The friar suggests Hero fakes her death; Leonato instructs Claudio to marry his niece (who is really Hero)

Trickery
后果，后果严重的是deception，不严重的是trickery
目的intention -> well-intended trickery VS evil-intended deception
Good results like BB come from a well-intended trickery, bad results like the conflicts during the wedding scene come from bad-intended deceptions.

Gender 性别

6. Gender
Differences between men and women
Examples related to gender norms and gender roles
Benedick’s sexist attitude towards women (1.1) - all women are deceitful and untrustworthy.
Hero is expected to do as her father dictates, even if it’s not her own desire. Her uncle, Antonio, reminds her of her duty to her father (2.1.44-45).
Claudio’s sexist idea that believes that Hero’s beauty is the problem, rather than blaming Don Pedro, for the alleged change in plan (2.1.67).
Don John and Borachio’s plan to ruin Hero’s reputation with slander and
lies hints at the society’s distrust of women (2.2.20-24; 30-46).
Leonato’s belief that his only child is guilty: no substantial proof has been
given, yet he doesn’t believe her innocence initially (4.1.119-142; 149-153).
Leonato blames Margaret for Hero’s ruin, even though Borachio says she
wasn’t involved (5.4.4-6).
Hero has no choice but to marry Claudio: even after he has slandered her, she accepts his dishonourable behaviour and marries him, as is her duty as a woman few options in the world of the play (5.4.63-64)
注意写：
当时的性别观念-女性是男性的归属品property
女性的贞洁胜过一切
剧里世界的所有男性都害怕被戴绿帽子

Literary Devices 文学手法

Motifs: Motifs are recurring structures, contrasts, and literary devices that help develop major themes.
Motif 是不断出现的结构、意象、场面、动作、符号或文学手法。
Symbols: Symbols are recurring objects, images, or details that carry deeper meanings beyond their literal sense.
Symbol 象征是通过具体物象承载更深层主题、人物心理或社会价值的文学手法。
Allusions: Allusions are brief references to mythological, biblical, or cultural figures that add meaning, irony, or thematic depth.
典故是对神话、圣经或文化人物的引用，用来增强讽刺、主题和人物塑造。
